Predicting Invasive Ductal Carcinoma using Deep CNNs

Collaboration with J.R. Harish Kumar, Manipal Institute of Technology
April '19 - June '19

Designed a deep convolution neural network architecture for detection and classification of invasive ductal carcinoma using whole slide images of breast cancer tissues. The network comprises of two convolutional layers, out of which the second one is followed by a max pooling layer along with two dropout and fully connected layers, respectively. The proposed methodology has been validated on a whole slide image dataset from 162 patients diagnosed with IDC. The experimental evaluation demonstrates promising quantitative results for automatic detection of invasive cancer tissues in terms of F1-score and balanced accuracy of 86.00% and 86.31%, respectively. The paper describing our work has been submitted for review to GlobalSIP 2019.

Cell Type Classification of RNA Sequenced Data Using ML Approaches

Collaboration with Dr. Sabyasachi Dasgupta, University of Toronto
Feb '19 - Present

Exploratory Research on using different autoencoder architectures to capture the representation of single-cell RNA-sequenced data in a confined latent space and clustering the cells to gene clusters using well-known algorithms to make a classification pipeline using a stacked autoencoder architecture, ultimately, trying to understand classification accuracy as a function of number of gene clusters. Preprocessing steps include cleaning of single cell data using filtering approaches with percentage of mitochondrial genes, total count of cells and using Dimensionality reduction with Autoencoders and PCA to perform marker analysis after clustering cells.

An Efficient Classification Scheme for Brain Computer Interface Using Extreme Gradient Boosting

Under The Supervision Of Dr. Amrita Chaturvedi, IIT-BHU
Dec '18 - March '19

We have performed signal smoothing to fix unregulated electroencephalogram (EEG) fluctuations and to effectively collect hidden patterns in corresponding EEG spectra. To do it, we apply a Savitzky Golay function and for feature selection, we applied the Filter Bank Common Spatial Pattern (FBCSP) algorithm with the Principle Component Analysis (PCA). These features are utilized in the classification process by the eXtreme Gradient Boosting (XGBoost) algorithm with suitable node split criteria to manage optimal tree height. Our approach enhances classification accuracy (88.80%) by approximately 10% over regularized common spatial patterns (78.01% accuracy), 15% over shift variance approximation (73.84% accuracy) and 15% over Riemannian approach (74.77% accuracy).
